Phishing Safety Tips

🔗

Never Click Unknown Links

Always hover over links to see the real destination. Phishers disguise malicious URLs with trusted-looking text like "Click here to verify".

📧

Verify Sender Identity

Check the actual email address, not just the display name. Attackers use addresses like support@paypa1.com instead of paypal.com.

⚡

Urgency Is a Red Flag

"Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!" Legitimate companies don't pressure you with artificial deadlines. Take time to verify.

🔒

HTTPS Alone Isn't Safe

Phishing sites use HTTPS too. Always check the full domain — secure-login.paypal.verify-now.com is NOT PayPal.

📱

Scan QR Codes Before Opening

QR codes can hide malicious URLs. Always preview the destination before opening. Use PhishGuard's QR scanner to stay safe.

🌐

New Domains Are Suspicious

Phishing sites are often created days before attacks. A domain under 180 days old is a strong warning signal — PhishGuard checks this.

🏦

Banks Never Ask for Passwords

No legitimate bank or government agency will ever ask for your password, PIN, or full SSN via email or SMS.

📎

Attachments Can Be Dangerous

Malware is often hidden in Office docs, PDFs, or ZIP files. Never open attachments from unknown senders without scanning first.
